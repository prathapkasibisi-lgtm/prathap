DELHI - CITY OVERVIEW
=======================

1. INTRODUCTION
----------------
Delhi is the capital territory of India.
It comprises New Delhi, the seat of the national government.
Delhi is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world.
The city has served as a capital for several historic empires.
It lies in the northern part of India near the Yamuna River.
Delhi is officially known as the National Capital Territory of Delhi.
Today it is a major political, cultural, and economic center.

2. HISTORY
----------
Delhi's history spans more than a thousand years.
It has been the capital of numerous dynasties and empires.
The Delhi Sultanate ruled from the city for several centuries.
The Mughal Empire later established Delhi as its capital.
Shah Jahan built the walled city of Shahjahanabad in the 1600s.
This area is known today as Old Delhi.
The British shifted their capital from Calcutta to Delhi in 1911.
New Delhi was inaugurated as the capital in 1931.
Edwin Lutyens designed much of New Delhi's colonial architecture.
After independence in 1947, Delhi became India's capital.
It gained special status as a National Capital Territory in 1991.

3. GEOGRAPHY
-------------
Delhi is located in northern India along the Yamuna River.
The city lies on the Indo-Gangetic Plain.
It shares borders with the states of Haryana and Uttar Pradesh.
Delhi experiences a humid subtropical climate.
Summers are extremely hot, often exceeding 45 degrees Celsius.
Winters are cold, with temperatures sometimes near freezing.
The monsoon season brings rainfall from July to September.
Air pollution is a significant environmental concern in Delhi.

4. CULTURE
----------
Delhi represents a blend of Mughal, colonial, and modern Indian culture.
The city has long been a center for arts, literature, and cuisine.
Hindi and Urdu poetry traditions flourished historically in Delhi.
Street food culture is vibrant, especially in Old Delhi's lanes.
Popular dishes include chaat, parathas, and butter chicken.
Delhi hosts numerous festivals including Diwali and Republic Day events.
The city is home to diverse religious and cultural communities.
Traditional markets like Chandni Chowk reflect centuries-old commerce.

5. LANDMARKS
------------
The Red Fort was the residence of Mughal emperors for centuries.
It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Old Delhi.
Qutub Minar is the tallest brick minaret in the world.
India Gate honors soldiers who died in World War I.
Humayun's Tomb inspired the design of the Taj Mahal.
Lotus Temple is known for its distinctive flower-shaped architecture.
Akshardham Temple showcases intricate Hindu architecture and exhibits.
Jama Masjid is one of the largest mosques in India.
Rashtrapati Bhavan is the official residence of India's President.
Connaught Place is a major commercial and business hub.

6. ECONOMY
----------
Delhi's economy is driven largely by services and trade.
The government sector employs a significant portion of the workforce.
Information technology and telecommunications are growing industries.
Delhi is a major hub for retail and wholesale trade.
The city also supports manufacturing in its surrounding regions.
Tourism contributes substantially to the local economy.
Real estate and construction sectors have expanded rapidly.
Delhi's per capita income ranks among the highest in India.

7. EDUCATION
------------
Delhi hosts several of India's most prestigious universities.
The University of Delhi is one of the largest in the country.
Jawaharlal Nehru University is known for research and humanities.
IIT Delhi is a leading institute for engineering education.
All India Institute of Medical Sciences is a top medical college.
Jamia Millia Islamia is a prominent central university.
Numerous schools and colleges serve the city's large population.

8. DEMOGRAPHICS
----------------
Delhi's metropolitan population exceeds thirty million people.
Hindi is the most widely spoken language in the city.
Punjabi, Urdu, and English are also commonly used.
Hinduism is the majority religion, followed by Islam and Sikhism.
Christians, Jains, and Buddhists also form smaller communities.
The city attracts migrants from every state in India.

9. TRANSPORTATION
------------------
Delhi Metro is one of the largest metro networks in the world.
It connects the city with neighboring regions like Noida and Gurugram.
Indira Gandhi International Airport is a major aviation hub.
Delhi has an extensive network of buses and auto-rickshaws.
Ring roads and expressways connect Delhi to nearby states.
Railway stations in Delhi serve as key hubs for national travel.

10. CONCLUSION
--------------
Delhi stands as a living museum of India's layered history.
Its monuments reflect centuries of dynastic rule and architecture.
As the national capital, it remains the political heart of India.
The city continues to grow as an economic and cultural center.
Delhi balances its ancient heritage with modern urban development.
