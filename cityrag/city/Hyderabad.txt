HYDERABAD - CITY OVERVIEW
==========================

1. INTRODUCTION
----------------
Hyderabad is the capital city of the Indian state of Telangana.
It is one of the largest cities in India by population.
The city is located on the Deccan Plateau in southern India.
Hyderabad is often called the "City of Pearls."
It is also known as "Cyberabad" due to its thriving IT industry.
The city was founded in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah.
It served as the capital of the princely state of Hyderabad.
Before 2014, it was the joint capital of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana.
Since 2014, it has been the sole capital of Telangana.
The city sits on the banks of the Musi River.

2. HISTORY
----------
Hyderabad was founded by the Qutb Shahi dynasty.
The dynasty ruled the Golconda Sultanate for nearly two centuries.
Golconda was famous for its diamond mines and trade.
The Kohinoor diamond is believed to have originated near Golconda.
In 1687, the Mughal Empire annexed the Golconda Sultanate.
Later, the Nizams of Hyderabad took control of the region.
The Nizams ruled Hyderabad State until Indian independence in 1947.
Hyderabad State was the largest princely state in India.
In 1948, the Indian Army carried out Operation Polo.
This operation led to the integration of Hyderabad into India.
In 1956, Hyderabad became the capital of Andhra Pradesh.
In 2014, Telangana was carved out as a separate state.
Hyderabad remained the capital, shared for ten years.
From 2024 onward, it is fully the capital of Telangana only.

3. GEOGRAPHY
-------------
Hyderabad lies at an average elevation of about 542 meters.
The city spans an area of roughly 650 square kilometers.
It is situated on rocky terrain typical of the Deccan Plateau.
Hussain Sagar Lake connects the twin cities of Hyderabad and Secunderabad.
The Musi River flows through the southern part of the city.
Numerous granite hills and boulders surround the urban landscape.
The climate is classified as a tropical wet and dry climate.
Summers are hot, while winters remain mild and pleasant.
Monsoon rains arrive between June and September.

4. CULTURE
----------
Hyderabad has a rich blend of Telugu and Islamic culture.
The city is known for its distinctive Hyderabadi dialect.
Hyderabadi cuisine is famous worldwide for its biryani.
Hyderabadi biryani is prepared using the dum cooking method.
Other popular dishes include haleem, kebabs, and irani chai.
Osmania biscuits are a beloved local tea-time snack.
The city celebrates festivals like Eid, Diwali, and Bonalu.
Bonalu is a folk festival dedicated to the goddess Mahakali.
Qawwali and Sufi music traditions remain popular here.
Pearl and bangle trading has long been part of local commerce.
The Laad Bazaar near Charminar is famous for bangles.

5. LANDMARKS
------------
Charminar is the most iconic monument in Hyderabad.
It was built in 1591 to commemorate the end of a plague.
Golconda Fort is a massive fortress with acoustic marvels.
The fort's design allows sound to travel great distances.
Qutb Shahi Tombs house the graves of the Qutb Shahi rulers.
Chowmahalla Palace was the seat of the Nizams' court.
Falaknuma Palace is a luxurious heritage hotel today.
The Birla Mandir is a prominent white marble temple.
Hussain Sagar Lake features a large Buddha statue at its center.
Ramoji Film City is one of the world's largest film studios.
Salar Jung Museum holds one of the largest art collections in India.

6. ECONOMY
----------
Hyderabad is a major hub for information technology in India.
HITEC City is the primary IT and business district.
Many global technology companies have offices in Hyderabad.
These include Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Facebook.
The city is also a center for pharmaceutical manufacturing.
It is often called the "Genome Valley" of India.
Bulk drug and vaccine production is significant here.
The pearl and diamond trade remains economically important.
Hyderabad also has a growing biotechnology sector.
Real estate and infrastructure development have boomed recently.
The Hyderabad Metro Rail serves as a key transit system.
Rajiv Gandhi International Airport connects the city globally.

7. EDUCATION
------------
Hyderabad hosts several prestigious educational institutions.
The University of Hyderabad is a central university.
Osmania University is one of the oldest in India.
IIIT Hyderabad focuses on information technology research.
NALSAR University of Law is a leading law school.
Indian School of Business is a top-ranked business school.
Many engineering and medical colleges operate in the city.

8. DEMOGRAPHICS
----------------
Hyderabad's population exceeds ten million in its metro area.
Telugu and Urdu are the two most widely spoken languages.
Hindi and English are also commonly used in business.
The city has a diverse religious composition.
Hindus and Muslims form the two largest religious communities.
Christians, Sikhs, and other communities also reside here.
People from across India migrate here for jobs and education.

9. TRANSPORTATION
------------------
The Hyderabad Metro Rail opened its first phase in 2017.
It is one of the largest metro rail systems in India.
The Outer Ring Road encircles the city for faster travel.
MMTS trains provide suburban rail connectivity.
Numerous highways connect Hyderabad to other major cities.
The airport is named after former Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi.

10. CONCLUSION
--------------
Hyderabad blends centuries of history with modern innovation.
Its heritage sites attract tourists from around the world.
Its IT corridor drives significant economic growth for India.
The city continues to expand while preserving its identity.
Hyderabad remains a symbol of unity between old and new India.
