BANGALORE - CITY OVERVIEW
===========================

1. INTRODUCTION
----------------
Bangalore is the capital city of the Indian state of Karnataka.
Its official name today is Bengaluru.
The city is located on the Deccan Plateau in southern India.
Bangalore is widely known as the "Silicon Valley of India."
It is one of the fastest-growing cities in the country.
The city sits at a high elevation compared to most Indian cities.
Bangalore has a long history dating back nearly 500 years.
Today it stands as a global center for technology and innovation.

2. HISTORY
----------
Bangalore was founded by Kempe Gowda I in 1537.
He built a mud fort that marked the city's beginning.
The city later came under the rule of the Vijayanagara Empire.
It then passed to the Mysore Kingdom and Hyder Ali's rule.
Tipu Sultan further developed the city's fortifications.
The British took control after the fourth Anglo-Mysore War.
Bangalore became a center for British cantonment areas.
After independence, it became the capital of Mysore State.
In 1973, Mysore State was renamed Karnataka.
Bangalore remained its capital and grew rapidly thereafter.
In 2014, the city's official name changed to Bengaluru.

3. GEOGRAPHY
-------------
Bangalore lies at an elevation of about 900 meters above sea level.
This high altitude gives it a relatively moderate climate.
The city is located in the southeastern part of Karnataka.
It does not lie on any major river, unlike many Indian cities.
Numerous man-made lakes dot the urban landscape.
The climate is classified as tropical savanna.
Summers remain mild compared to other Indian cities.
Winters are cool and pleasant throughout the season.
Monsoon rains occur mainly from June to September.

4. CULTURE
----------
Bangalore blends traditional Kannada culture with cosmopolitan influences.
The city has a large population from across India and abroad.
Kannada is the official language of the state and city.
Classical music and Carnatic traditions remain culturally significant.
The city hosts numerous pubs, cafes, and nightlife venues.
It has earned the nickname "Pub Capital of India."
Local cuisine includes dishes like bisi bele bath and ragi mudde.
Masala dosa is believed to have strong ties to the region.
Filter coffee is a beloved daily beverage across the city.
Bangalore celebrates festivals like Karaga and Ugadi widely.

5. LANDMARKS
------------
Lalbagh Botanical Garden is a historic garden founded by Hyder Ali.
Cubbon Park offers a large green space in the city center.
Bangalore Palace resembles Tudor-style architecture from England.
Tipu Sultan's Summer Palace showcases Indo-Islamic architecture.
Vidhana Soudha houses the state legislature building.
ISKCON Temple attracts many devotees and visitors each year.
Nandi Hills lies just outside the city as a popular getaway.
Wonderla amusement park is a popular family entertainment spot.
Bannerghatta National Park houses a zoo and wildlife safari.

6. ECONOMY
----------
Bangalore is India's leading hub for information technology.
It hosts major campuses of global tech companies.
These include Infosys, Wipro, IBM, and Microsoft.
The city is also a center for biotechnology research.
Aerospace and defense industries have a strong presence here.
Hindustan Aeronautics Limited is headquartered in Bangalore.
The Indian Space Research Organisation has facilities in the city.
Bangalore also supports a thriving startup ecosystem.
Numerous venture capital firms invest in local startups.
The city contributes significantly to India's software exports.

7. EDUCATION
------------
Bangalore hosts many premier educational and research institutions.
The Indian Institute of Science is a top research university.
IIM Bangalore is one of India's leading business schools.
National Law School of India University is based here.
Bangalore University serves a large student population.
Numerous engineering and medical colleges operate citywide.

8. DEMOGRAPHICS
----------------
Bangalore's metropolitan population exceeds twelve million people.
Kannada is the most widely spoken local language.
English is extensively used in business and daily life.
Tamil, Telugu, and Hindi speakers form large communities too.
Hinduism is the majority religion, with Islam and Christianity following.
The city attracts professionals and students from across India.

9. TRANSPORTATION
------------------
Namma Metro serves as Bangalore's rapid transit rail system.
Kempegowda International Airport connects the city worldwide.
The city has an extensive network of state-run buses.
Suburban rail projects aim to ease commuter congestion.
Traffic congestion remains a significant challenge for the city.
Ring roads and flyovers have been built to improve connectivity.

10. CONCLUSION
--------------
Bangalore stands as India's foremost technology and innovation hub.
Its parks, palaces, and gardens reflect a rich historical past.
The city continues to attract talent from across the globe.
Bangalore balances rapid urban growth with green spaces.
It remains a model for India's modern economic aspirations.
