CHENNAI - CITY OVERVIEW
=========================

1. INTRODUCTION
----------------
Chennai is the capital city of the Indian state of Tamil Nadu.
It was formerly known as Madras.
The city is located on the Coromandel Coast of the Bay of Bengal.
Chennai is one of the largest metropolitan cities in India.
It is often called the "Gateway to South India."
The city has a rich history dating back over 300 years.
Chennai was established by the British East India Company in 1639.
Today it is a major cultural, economic, and educational center.

2. HISTORY
----------
The British founded Fort St. George in Chennai in 1644.
This fort became the first British stronghold in India.
Madras grew as an important trading post for the British.
The city served as the capital of the Madras Presidency.
Various dynasties like the Cholas and Pallavas ruled the region earlier.
Mahabalipuram, near Chennai, was a major Pallava port city.
After independence, Madras Presidency became Madras State.
In 1996, the city was officially renamed Chennai.
The name change reflected the city's Tamil cultural heritage.

3. GEOGRAPHY
-------------
Chennai lies on the flat coastal plains of Tamil Nadu.
The city is bordered by the Bay of Bengal to the east.
The Cooum and Adyar rivers flow through the city.
Chennai has an average elevation close to sea level.
The climate is classified as tropical wet and dry.
Summers are extremely hot and humid in Chennai.
The northeast monsoon brings heavy rains between October and December.
Chennai is prone to cyclones due to its coastal location.

4. CULTURE
----------
Chennai is considered the cultural capital of Tamil Nadu.
Bharatanatyam, a classical dance form, has strong roots here.
Carnatic music is deeply embedded in the city's culture.
The Margazhi season features numerous music and dance festivals.
Tamil cinema, known as Kollywood, is based in Chennai.
Filmmaking has been a significant industry since the early 1900s.
Chennai's traditional attire includes the veshti and saree.
Local cuisine includes idli, dosa, sambar, and filter coffee.
Chettinad cuisine, known for its spice, is also popular here.
Pongal is one of the most important festivals celebrated locally.

5. LANDMARKS
------------
Marina Beach is one of the longest urban beaches in the world.
Fort St. George houses a museum and government offices today.
Kapaleeshwarar Temple is a significant Shiva temple in Mylapore.
San Thome Basilica is built over the tomb of St. Thomas the Apostle.
The Government Museum in Egmore holds rare archaeological artifacts.
Vivekananda House stands where Swami Vivekananda once stayed.
Guindy National Park is a protected green space within the city.
Mahabalipuram's shore temples lie just south of Chennai.
Valluvar Kottam honors the Tamil poet Thiruvalluvar.

6. ECONOMY
----------
Chennai is a major center for the automobile industry in India.
It is often referred to as the "Detroit of India."
Many automobile manufacturers have factories near Chennai.
The city is also a hub for information technology services.
The IT corridor along OMR hosts numerous tech companies.
Chennai Port is one of the busiest ports in the country.
The healthcare industry is strong, earning the city a "Health Capital" title.
Textile and leather industries also contribute to the local economy.
Financial services and banking have a significant presence here.

7. EDUCATION
------------
Chennai hosts several top-ranked educational institutions.
IIT Madras is one of India's premier engineering institutes.
Anna University focuses on technical and engineering education.
University of Madras is one of the oldest universities in India.
Madras Medical College is a leading medical institution.
Many arts, science, and law colleges operate throughout the city.

8. DEMOGRAPHICS
----------------
Chennai's metropolitan population exceeds ten million people.
Tamil is the predominant language spoken in the city.
English is widely used in business and education.
Telugu, Malayalam, and Hindi speakers also live in Chennai.
Hinduism is the majority religion, followed by Islam and Christianity.
The city attracts migrants from across South India for work.

9. TRANSPORTATION
------------------
Chennai Metro Rail connects key parts of the city efficiently.
Chennai Central and Egmore are major railway stations.
Chennai International Airport serves as a key aviation hub.
The Mass Rapid Transit System serves southern parts of the city.
An extensive bus network is operated by the state transport corporation.
Chennai Port and Ennore Port support maritime trade activities.

10. CONCLUSION
--------------
Chennai combines deep cultural roots with modern industry.
Its beaches, temples, and classical arts attract many visitors.
The automobile and IT sectors continue to drive its growth.
Chennai remains a vital gateway to South India's economy and culture.
The city balances tradition and modernity in its everyday life.
